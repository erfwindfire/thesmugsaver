# Energy Bills Rising October 2025: 10 Ways to Reduce Your £1,755 Annual Cost

### Key Points
*   **The Forecast**: The Energy Price Cap has risen to £1,755 (annual average). It is expected to remain high through Winter 2025 due to global gas market volatility.
*   **The Fix**: A standard "Variable" tariff is bleeding money. Fixed tariffs are currently ~5% cheaper than the cap.
*   **The "Heating the Human" strategy**: It costs 3p/hour to run a heated throw vs £1.50/hour to heat a whole house.
*   **The Water Hack**: Reducing shower flow rates saves more on *gas* (heating water) than water usage.

We are entering the fourth winter of the energy crisis. While we aren't at the terrifying £4,000 peaks of 2022, £1,755 is still double the historic norm.

For the average household, energy is now the second biggest bill after Housing.

The "Price Cap" is not a cap on your total bill; it is a cap on the unit rate. If you use more, you pay more. Here is how to fight back.

## Strategy 1: The Tariff Switch (Save £100)
**Do not sit on the Standard Variable Tariff.**
*   **Action**: Check MoneySavingExpert or Uswitch.
*   **Target**: Look for a **1-Year Fix** that is priced *below* the current Price Cap. Currently, fixes exist that are 5-7% cheaper.
*   **Risk**: If global prices collapse, you are stuck paying more. But "Price Certainty" has a value.

## Strategy 2: Tweak the Combi Boiler (Save £80)
If you have a Combi boiler (no hot water tank):
1.  Go to the boiler unit.
2.  Find the dial with a "Radiator" icon (Flow Temperature).
3.  Turn it down from 75°C to **60°C**.
*   **Why**: Combi boilers only condense (work efficiently) at lower temps. At 75°C, you are sending heat straight out the flue. Your house will still get warm; it just takes 10 mins longer.

## Strategy 3: The "Vampire" Hunt (Save £40)
Devices in standby mode cost money.
*   **Culprits**: Games Consoles (Xbox "Instant On" is a power hog), Smart Speakers, TVs.
*   **Action**: Use smart plugs to kill power to the TV/Console stack at night automatically.

## Strategy 4: The Shower Head Swap (Save £50 Gas + £50 Water)
*   **Audit**: Put a bucket under your shower. If it fills in <40 seconds, you are pouring money down the drain.
*   **Fix**: Buy an "Aerated" shower head (£15). It mixes air with water. The pressure feels the same, but it uses 40% less water. Less water = less gas needed to heat it.

## FAQ: Energy Bills

### Should I fix my energy prices until 2026?
If you value certainty over gambling, **Yes**. The predictions for 2026 are flat. A fix locks in your budget and protects you from a sudden geopolitical shock spiking gas prices again.

### What about Smart Meters?
Get one. Not for the "In Home Display" (which is depressing), but to access **Smart Tariffs**. Tariffs like "Agile Octopus" offer half-price electricity when it's windy. You cannot get these deals with an old meter.

### Is the "Warm Home Discount" still a thing?
Yes. It is £150 off your bill if you are on Pension Credit or certain means-tested benefits. It is usually applied automatically, but check your supplier has the right data.
